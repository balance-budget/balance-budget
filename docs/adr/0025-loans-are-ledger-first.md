# Loans are ledger-first: account subtrees, loan-managed posting, computed-only schedules

A **Loan** (mortgage, personal loan — not mortgage-specific) is a thin domain layer *over* the ledger, never a parallel bookkeeping system. Every Loan is one non-postable **Liability** parent Account with one postable child Account per **Loan Part** (Dutch *leningdeel*) — always, even for single-part loans, so splitting a part later (rate renewal, renovation) is just adding a child instead of restructuring a postable account with history (ADR-0019). A part's Account is created fresh or *adopted* (an existing postable Liability leaf re-parented under the loan, history intact), which is the only clean onboarding path for a mortgage that already has posted entries. The part's outstanding principal **is** its Account balance; the loan domain never stores a competing figure.

Part Accounts are **Loan-managed**: generic flows (manual journal entry, Reassign, plain categorisation) refuse to target them — only loan-aware flows post. Amounts, however, stay user-editable inside those flows: the engine's split is a pre-filled *proposal*, and the bank's actual charge wins over the calculation. This inverts the usual direction — the schedule reconciles to posted reality, never the reverse — and removes any need for drift detection between schedule and ledger. Monthly payments arrive through the existing categorisation flow (ADR-0013): the Loan references its lender Counterparty, the Inbox surfaces a loan-payment hint, and the posted entry carries one principal line per amortizing part plus one interest line per part (all to the Loan's single configured interest Expense Account), each counter-line tagged with its Loan Part so per-part actuals — interest included — are a pure ledger query.

The amortization schedule is a **pure function, never a table**: (part definitions, effective-dated rate periods, current ledger balances, horizon) → projected periods. There is nothing to invalidate when a payment posts, a rate changes, or an entry is deleted. This works because the engine computes from an **anchor snapshot** — outstanding balance now, rate now, months to end date — not by replaying from inception. For an annuity that is mathematically equivalent to the original schedule, and it makes an **extra repayment** need zero event machinery under the Dutch-default bank policy (end date fixed, payment recalculated): the balance drops, the next computation *is* the recalculation. Term-shortening exists only as a what-if lever in the (server-side, ephemeral) simulator. Rate changes are appended effective-dated facts, not replayed events.

Rejected: inception replay (onboarding a 10-year-old mortgage would mean archaeology through old statements, and any missed event desynchronises schedule and ledger forever — consequence: no synthetic pre-anchor history is drawn for adopted loans; the graph starts where ledger data starts); a materialized schedule table (a cache with regeneration duties on every posting, edit, and rate change, bought for queries nothing needs); a loan-domain payment-breakdown side table (the same numbers in two places, desynchronisable by an Uncleared-line edit); hard-locking engine-computed amounts (a €0.02 bank rounding difference would be unrecordable); auto-posting expected entries via Quartz with bank-row attach (mismatch handling needs amend machinery and stretches the Attach predicate of ADR-0012 beyond self-transfers); soft warn-only ownership of part accounts (the invariant "part balance = what the loan did" would be unenforced, requiring exactly the drift detection this design deletes).
