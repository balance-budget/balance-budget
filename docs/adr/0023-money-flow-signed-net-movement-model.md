# Money flow renders every account as a signed Net-movement flow, side-by-sign

The **Money flow** **Report** (the Sankey at `/api/reports/flow`) is built from one number per **Account** — its **Net movement** over the **Reporting period** — rather than by tracing actual money paths through the ledger. Every postable **Account** contributes exactly one flow whose width is `|Net movement|`; its side is chosen by sign under the existing **Sign convention**: money *in* on the source side (**Income**, plus balance-sheet **Accounts** that shrank — a drained savings account, new borrowing on a card) and money *out* on the exit side (**Expense**, plus balance-sheet **Accounts** that grew — savings, investments, debt paid down, cash left as a buffer). All sources converge into a single hub and fan back out to all exits. The diagram balances exactly — and with no fudge node — because of the double-entry identity `Σ Income − Σ Expense = Σ (balance-sheet Net movement)`: the surplus or deficit is simply *where the balance sheet moved*, decomposed by account. A net-negative **Account** flips to the opposite side for that period (a refund-heavy **Expense** behaves like income; a clawed-back **Income** behaves like spend). The **Income** / **Expense** sides render at full **Chart of accounts** depth (subtrees become intermediate Sankey columns); balance-sheet **Accounts** render only at top level in v1.

## Considered options

- **(A, chosen) Per-account signed Net movement, side-by-sign, single hub.** Needs only the same period-aggregate the **Distribution** already computes, extended from **Income** / **Expense** to all five **AccountType**s. Cheap (one `GROUP BY` over the period), always balances, and never claims a false pairing between a specific income source and a specific outlay — which money's fungibility makes genuinely unknowable. Surfaces the personal-finance insight people actually want: investments, savings, and debt paydown appear as their own exit nodes; a drained account or new borrowing appears as a source.
- **(B, rejected) Ledger-true flow tracing.** Make the user's **Asset** / **Liability** **Accounts** the middle column and draw real `Income → account → Expense` flows by following each **JournalEntry**'s contra legs. More literally truthful, but it forces proportional flow-attribution across splits and multi-leg entries, and it implies an income→expense pairing that does not exist (money is fungible). The attribution complexity buys no extra honesty over (A) — it manufactures false precision. Reserved as a possible future "advanced flow" view, not v1.

## Consequences

- The surplus/deficit is never a single anonymous "Savings" plug; it is always decomposed into the specific balance-sheet **Accounts** that moved. This is the feature, not an accident — it is what lets "money into investments" read as a distinct exit.
- Top-level-only balance-sheet rendering in v1 is a deliberate cut: an **Asset** / **Liability** subtree can have **mixed-sign children** (Checking drained −, Brokerage grew +), and a parent placeholder node cannot sit on both the source and exit side at once. **Income** / **Expense** subtrees are effectively single-signed, so nesting them is safe. Deep balance-sheet nesting is deferred until that mixed-sign layout question is worth solving.
- If a **Reporting period** spans onboarding, the seeded `Opening Balances` **Equity** **Account** shows as a large source node. That is correct (it is where the starting money came from) and self-labelling, so it is intentionally not special-cased.
- The rule that makes the diagram balance (`Σ Income − Σ Expense = Σ balance-sheet Net movement`) is the invariant to assert in tests; if it ever fails to hold, the bug is in **Net movement** computation, not in the Sankey layout.
