# Journal overview amount sign reflects net worth change

The `/journal` overview shows one signed `Money` per **JournalEntry**, derived from its effect on net worth: `sign(amount) = sign(Σ ΔAssets − Σ ΔLiabilities)` over the entry's **JournalLines**, magnitude the absolute value of that sum. When the sum is non-zero (an entry touching Income, Expense, or Equity) the row renders signed and colored (negative `text-danger`, positive `text-success`). When the sum is zero — the entry only shuffles between Assets and Liabilities (Asset↔Asset transfer, credit-card payment, loan disbursement) — the row renders the gross debit magnitude unsigned and muted, signalling "transfer, not gain or loss." Splits follow the same rule.

We rejected signing from a canonical "from" leg (no such leg exists for multi-source splits, and it makes transfers misleadingly directional) and account-type heuristics (they bifurcate into untenable combinatorics). The projection lives client-side in `src/lib/journalProjection.ts`; the `/api/journal-entries` endpoint stays bookkeeping-shaped, per ADR-0007.
