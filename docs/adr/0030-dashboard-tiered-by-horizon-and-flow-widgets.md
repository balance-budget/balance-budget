# Dashboard splits balance trends by Horizon, stacks accounts, and trades per-account registers for flow widgets

The dashboard's single account-balance trend chart overlaid every liquid **Asset** account as a line. In real data the lines differ by orders of magnitude — a Savings balance runs 10–100× a Current account, real estate 100× — so the large series flatten the small ones into noise at the bottom of the axis, defeating the chart's purpose ("how is my day-to-day money trending?"). The fix is not a better single chart but **separating accounts by order of magnitude / time horizon onto their own axes**, plus retiring a low-value widget for ones other personal-finance tools have converged on.

## A new Horizon axis, orthogonal to Liquidity

We introduce **Horizon** — a per-**Account** property on **Asset** and **Liability** accounts, one of **Short-term**, **Medium-term**, **Long-term** — defined in `CONTEXT.md`. It answers "*when* do I expect to draw on this money?" (today / this year / this decade), which correlates with magnitude. It is deliberately **a separate axis from Liquidity**, not a third value bolted onto `IsLiquid`: the user's own framing keeps Savings *Liquid* (it still counts toward **Liquid net worth**), so the thing that distinguishes Current from Savings is horizon, not liquidity. Keeping them orthogonal also lets the awkward cases stay expressible — a locked five-year deposit is **Medium-term *and* Illiquid**; the house is **Long-term *and* Illiquid** — which a single three-valued `Liquidity` enum could not represent and which would have forced a redefinition of what **Liquid net worth** sums. This vindicates rather than violates the existing Liquidity glossary note, which already refused to let the short-/long-term *maturity* dimension pollute Liquidity; that dimension now has its own home, and the two entries cross-reference.

Horizon is **defaulted on account creation** — Illiquid → Long-term; Liquid with a linked **Savings** **BankAccount** → Medium-term; otherwise Short-term — then stored and freely editable, so a no-bank-feed liquid account (cash, a brokerage cash balance) and a locked deposit can both be corrected by hand. Like Liquidity, Horizon is exempt from the subtree homogeneity rule and never touches accounting semantics; it is a presentation/grouping axis only. This is the one new ledger column the decision introduces.

## Three charts, two archetypes

The dashboard renders one chart per Horizon tier, but in two shapes:

- **Short-term and Medium-term** charts are **per-account stacked areas** over a short, daily range (1M/3M/6M). Stacking shows composition — each account is a band, the top edge is the tier total (total spending money / total reserves). Because a Current account can occasionally go into overdraft, the stack uses Recharts' **`stackOffset="sign"`**: positive balances stack upward from zero, negative balances stack downward, so an overdraft dips honestly below the axis instead of corrupting the "total = top edge" reading. Per-account trajectory is sacrificed as the *primary* reading (legend/hover still expose it) — acceptable, because the swamping problem is already solved by the tier split; stacking is a composition bonus, not the fix.

- The **Long-term** chart is not per-account. It plots **Net worth and Liquid net worth as two aggregate lines** (the two figures already in `DashboardSummaryOutput`) over a **monthly** cadence and a long range (1Y/3Y/All). The gap between the two lines *is* illiquid net worth — house value amortizing against mortgage debt — which is exactly the decade-horizon story real estate tells, without needing a per-account chart of fixed assets. Monthly sampling is mandatory here: a decade of daily points is both heavy and noise, and the house line is legitimately a near-flat step function that only moves on revaluation entries and monthly loan payments.

## Flow widgets replace the per-account registers

The "last *N* transactions per account" panel (the Firefly-III layout) is dropped. A survey of Monarch, Copilot, Empower, and Simplifi shows none of them fragment recent activity by account; the questions a dashboard is actually asked are "where did my money go?", "what's coming?", and "am I net-positive?". All three new balance charts are **stock** views, so the dashboard also lacked any **flow** view beyond two month-to-date numbers. We keep the account **balance list** (avatar, name, current balance — still useful) and remove only the embedded transaction previews, then fill the reclaimed space with two flow widgets, both backed by existing services:

- **Spending by category — this month.** The **top *N* leaf Expense accounts** for the month-to-date with an "Other" tail bucket, via `IReportsService.GetDistributionAsync(type: Expense, …)` (slices already sorted largest-first). Leaf-level, not rolled-up roots, was chosen for concreteness ("Rent / Groceries / Dining" reads better than "Housing / Food") at the cost of some month-to-month stability.

- **Upcoming — rest of this month.** A single chronological feed of recurring items still due before month-end, flattened across accounts from `IOutlookService`'s existing `OutlookThisMonthOutput.Items`, led by the projected month-end **Liquid net worth** (`EndBalanceMid`). It reuses the Outlook engine as-is — **ADR-0028's current-month anchor** — so it adds no engine logic, accepting that the feed thins toward month-end; a "nothing left this month — see Outlook" empty state covers the tail.

Both widgets need only a thin dashboard aggregation endpoint (flatten Outlook items across accounts; pick a this-month Expense distribution), not new computation.

Rejected / deferred: **splitting by `BankAccount.Type`** (the original request) — `BankAccount` is a one-to-one *optional* link carrying import metadata, so keying a dashboard layout on it strands every liquid account with no bank feed and has no home for the third `Card` type; Horizon covers every account and is ledger-native. **Promoting `Liquidity` to three values** — conflates two axes, forces redefining **Liquid net worth**, and cannot model an illiquid reserve. **A cash-flow trend widget** (income vs expense by month) — a strong staple and a natural future addition, but not chosen for this pass to keep the dashboard from bloating past three charts plus two widgets. **A rolling "next 30 days" Upcoming** — never goes empty but needs new cross-month template expansion (the engine's `RemainingOccurrences` is month-bounded); deferred until the month-end thinning proves annoying. **A unified recent-activity feed** and **budget-progress widgets** — the former is backward-looking like the panel it would replace; the latter depends on budgets, still deferred.
