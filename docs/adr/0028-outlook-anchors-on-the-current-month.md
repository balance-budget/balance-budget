# Outlook anchors on the current month and leads with concrete this-month / year-end answers

Amends ADR-0027 (the liquid **Projection** stays computed-never-stored, template-driven, with **Occurrence matching** and **Typical spend** unchanged in substance). This ADR changes only *where the projection starts* and *how it is presented*, because the first rendering answered the wrong question: it began the forward curve at the *next* month (`fromMonth = anchorMonth.AddMonths(1)`), collapsed everything into one net balance line, and wrapped that line in a `Typical spend` band — so a user asking "what's still going out **this** month, and what's left after it?" got no answer, and adding a recurring item appeared to change nothing (a constant monthly commitment shifts the band's mid and bounds equally, so it cancels out visually — see ADR-0027's `Typical spend` exclusion).

We decide the liquid **Projection** anchors on the **current month as a partial month**: the first projected period runs from *today* to month-end, counting only template occurrences still **due after today** (earlier ones are already in the current balance) and prorating the `Typical spend` band by the fraction of the month remaining. The **Outlook** screen leads with two concrete figures for the selected Account — **This month** (expected money still coming in, money still going out — recurring items shown *exactly* and split by direction — plus an everyday-spend range, yielding an end-of-month balance range) and **Year-end** (the projected balance on 31 December of the current year, computed by projecting through December regardless of the 6/12/24 horizon toggle) — over a chart that now starts at the current month and splits in/out. **Uncertainty attaches only to the un-templated everyday-spend residual**, rendered as a plain low–high range that carries through to the end-of-month and year-end figures; known commitments carry none. This is the ADR-0027 `Typical spend` band, narrowed to the one quantity that is genuinely uncertain and stated in plain numbers rather than a shaded ribbon around the whole curve.

Rejected / deferred: **keeping the next-month start** (simpler — whole months only, no proration — but it structurally cannot answer the two questions the section exists for). **A separate "this month" computation path** beside the month-by-month engine (duplicates the occurrence/spend logic and risks drift; instead the engine gains a partial-first-month mode that the cards read from its first row). **A whole-curve uncertainty band** (the original rendering — it makes every commitment look uncertain and hides that templating a known amount *removes* uncertainty rather than shifting a band). **Re-weighting / smoothing the `Typical spend` estimator itself** (the trailing 3-month min/median/max is crude, but how wide the range should be is a tuning detail orthogonal to this decision and left for later). No ledger columns and no stored projection are introduced — ADR-0027's "computed, never stored" invariant holds.
