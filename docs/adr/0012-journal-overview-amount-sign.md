# Journal overview row amount sign reflects net worth change

The `/journal` overview renders one row per **JournalEntry**, and the row's amount column shows a signed `Money` derived from the entry's effect on the user's net worth: `sign(amount) = sign(Σ ΔAssets − Σ ΔLiabilities)` over the entry's **JournalLines**, with the magnitude being the absolute value of that sum. When the sum is non-zero — the typical "operating" entry that touches Income, Expense, or Equity in addition to a balance-sheet leg — the row renders the amount signed (negative in `text-danger`, positive in `text-success`). When the sum is zero — i.e. the entry only shuffles money between Assets and Liabilities (Asset↔Asset transfer, credit-card payment, loan disbursement, drawdown) — the row renders the gross debit magnitude unsigned and muted, with no red/green colouring, signalling "this is a transfer, not a gain or loss." Split entries fall under the same rule; only when an entry has no single "from" leg do we degrade the secondary `from → to` label to "Split (N)" and let the user expand to see the breakdown — the sign of the amount column is unaffected.

We rejected (a) **magnitude-only display**, where the row shows only `|Σ debits|` and direction is conveyed solely by the `from → to` label, because it loses the at-a-glance "spend vs earn" cue that a mailbox-style chronological scan depends on and that **Account**-scoped registers already use; (b) **sign from the "from" leg's perspective**, where the from-leg's signed effect determines the sign, because it requires picking a canonical "from" leg even for entries whose shape doesn't have one (multi-source-multi-destination splits, Income↔Expense oddities) and because Asset↔Asset transfers come out unambiguously negative or positive depending on tiebreaker, which is misleading — a Checking → Savings transfer is neither a loss nor a gain; (c) **`accountTypeOutput`-driven heuristics** that interpret each leg type-by-type and produce a "principal account" view, because the rule then bifurcates by account-type combinatorics (Asset↔Expense vs Liability↔Expense vs Asset↔Liability, …) and every combinatoric corner needs a verdict that's defensible from a user-facing perspective. The net-worth-change rule is the only convention that simultaneously (i) matches the user's intuition — groceries are a loss, salary is a gain, moving money between own accounts is neither — (ii) is uniform across simple, split, and exotic entry shapes, and (iii) doesn't require picking a privileged leg. The cost we accept is that loan disbursements and credit-card payments render as muted transfers even though cash is genuinely moving in or out of the user's accounts: that's intentional, because net-worth-wise nothing changed, and the underlying balance-sheet movement is visible in the **Register** for each touched **Account**. The projection is implemented client-side in `src/lib/journalProjection.ts` because the journal overview is the sole consumer and the SPA already has the per-account `AccountType` cached via `useAccounts()`; the canonical `/api/journal-entries` endpoint stays bookkeeping-shaped (header + lines), and ADR-0008's UI-shaped server projection pattern remains reserved for the per-Account **Register**.
