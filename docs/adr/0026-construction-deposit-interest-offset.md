# Construction deposit: a plain asset account, with interest recorded as an offset inside the loan payment

A **Construction deposit** (Dutch *bouwdepot*) is mortgage money the lender has not yet disbursed — funds earmarked for building or renovation, drawn down as invoices arrive. We model it as a **plain Asset Account**, *not* **Loan-managed**: draw-downs are ordinary flows (a manual **JournalEntry** when the lender pays the contractor directly, or a **Self-transfer** when the lender reimburses you into Checking). The **Loan** only *references* it — via three optional, set-together fields (`ConstructionDepositAccountId`, `ConstructionDepositInterestIncomeAccountId`, `ConstructionDepositAnnualRatePercent`) — and never manages it.

During construction the lender charges interest on the full loan but credits interest on the deposit, and collects a **single netted debit**. We record that faithfully by adding a **Deposit-interest offset** — one **Income** line of `deposit balance at period start × monthly deposit rate`, capped at the entry's gross interest — to the loan-aware **Loan payment** proposal, so the entry's net equals the actual debit. The consequence worth flagging: the deposit interest is *consumed as compensation* and **never lands in the deposit balance**, which only shrinks via draws. The offset carries no **Loan Part** attribution (it is a loan-level line), and summary "current payment" figures show the net (gross − offset), reverting to gross automatically once the deposit drains to €0.

Rejected: **deposit interest as a separate accrual that grows the deposit balance** while the loan payment stays gross — contradicts the single netted debit the bank actually collects, and would require a second monthly entry plus a transfer to reconcile. Making the deposit **Loan-managed** — draws are plain ledger activity needing no loan-awareness, so management would only add friction. A **stored/effective-dated deposit rate** — the construction phase is short and the rate is an editable proposal default anyway (the bank's actual charge wins), so a single editable rate suffices. Applying a **leftover deposit balance against the loan** at completion is deferred: it is a once-per-loan event that would need the **Extra repayment** flow to accept a non-bank funding source, and is out of scope until it recurs.
