# Development sample data is seeded at runtime, rebuilt every startup

Development sample data is built at runtime through EF Core's `UseAsyncSeeding` hook (configured in `BalanceDbContext.OnConfiguring` after `UseProvider`, invoked by the startup `MigrateAsync`), guarded by `IHostEnvironment.IsDevelopment()` (which also excludes the `IntegrationTest` and design-time hosts). It wipes the previous sample ledger and rebuilds it with every **JournalEntry** date re-anchored to `TimeProvider`'s "today", so time-relative views are never empty. All of it lives in `Balance.Data/Seeding/`. This is unlike the **Reference data** seeded via `HasData` (Currencies, the `Opening Balances` Equity account), whose values are frozen at migration-add time and ship to production.

The wipe-and-rebuild deletes JournalLines, JournalEntries, BankTransactions + metadata, BankAccounts, Counterparties, and all Accounts **except `Opening Balances`** on every Development startup; the Currency reference rows, the `Opening Balances` Account, and Identity users are explicitly excluded and survive. Hand edits to seeded rows do not survive a restart. The seeder bypasses the service layer, so its rows must be hand-correct against the invariants services normally enforce: every entry's lines sum to zero per the **Sign convention**, and an attached BankTransaction carries `JournalEntryId` plus a bank-side JournalLine flipped to `Cleared` whose `Amount` equals the transaction's `Money.Amount` (raw-equality match, ADR-0012). A known `dev@balance.local` user is seeded create-if-absent.

We rejected `HasData` (ships to production, frozen dates) and a bespoke seeder service beside `MigrateDatabaseAsync` (abandons the EF hook for a second "seed the database" site).
